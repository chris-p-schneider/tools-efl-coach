List Info

I've designed the text input process to make it easy to copy/paste from websites or word documents with little tedious manual clean-up. When you input text into a new list, a few things happen:

  1. First, your list gets checked as to whether it uses commas, tabs, or line breaks to separate words. Please use one of these:
    • [,] Comma: vocabulary, words, like this
    • [ ] Tab: words separated by tabs (not spaces)
    • [\n] New line:
      • Words
      • separated
      • this way
  2. Next, lone single characters [like 'a'] are removed from your text. Some still might get through, so it's necessary to double-check afterwards by clicking "Edit".
  3. Then, non-English characters, numbers, and symbols are removed. There are a few exceptions, including a normal space:
    • ['] Apostrophe: doctor's
    • [!] Exclamation: Wow!
    • [?] Question: Really?
    • [.] Period: Mt. Everest
    • [-] Hyphen: twenty-one
    I decided on these characters because I think they are most likely to be used within vocabulary. Please note that all words are CaSe Sensitive. Capitalization matters.
  4. Next, the text is filtered again for single characters that might have been created when the non-letters were removed.
  5. Additionally, lowercase i' is reformatted to capital I'.
  6. Lastly, your list is saved with the above changes. I use line breaks as my delimiter, so your final list will be reformatted as such. When you edit your list, it will not be reformatted again.
